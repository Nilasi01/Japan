About

GEOGRAPHY OF JAPAN

• 73% of land is mountainous and hilly, 12.5% is used for agriculture
• 145,936 sq. mi. (slightly smaller than California)
• A mostly temperate climate with four distinct seasons
• Comprised of four large islands, Hokkaido, Honshu, Shikoku, Kyushu, and other smaller islands
• Has 47 prefectures
• Largest cities by population: Tokyo (Capital), Yokohama, Osaka, Nagoya, and Sapporo

LEGISLATURE

  • DIET

    Highest organization of state power & the sole law-making organ of the State.

  • EXECUTIVE

    Supreme decision-making organ of the executive branch.
    Consists of the Prime Minister and not more than 17 Ministers of State (and is collectively responsible to the Diet).
    The Prime Minister appoints or dismisses Ministers.

  • JUDICIARY

    Highest judicial organ Justice & 14 Justices

HISTORY OF JAPAN

• Ancient civilization dating back thousands of years
• Influenced by Chinese, Korean, and Western cultures over centuries
• Known for the Samurai era, Meiji Restoration, and post-WWII modernization

JAPANESE CULTURE

• Rich traditions like tea ceremonies, calligraphy, and ikebana (flower arrangement)
• Unique fashion, architecture, and festivals (e.g., Cherry Blossom Festival)
• World-famous cuisine such as sushi, ramen, and tempura

FUN FACTS

  • • Japan has more than 6800 islands
  • • Vending machines are found almost everywhere
  • • Home to the world's busiest train station: Shinjuku Station

TOP TRAVEL DESTINATIONS

• Tokyo - Modern metropolis with ancient temples
• Kyoto - Historic city with over 1,000 temples
• Mount Fuji - Iconic natural wonder
• Nara - Famous for deer park and Great Buddha

Basic Phrases - මූලික වාක්‍ය

  • こんにちは (Konnichiwa) - හෙලෝ
  • ありがとう (Arigatou) - ස්තුතියි
  • すみません (Sumimasen) - සමාවෙන්න / කණගාටුයි
  • おはよう (Ohayou) - සුභ උදෑසනක්
  • こんばんは (Konbanwa) - සුභ සැන්දෑවක්
